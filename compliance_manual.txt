CHAPTER 1: DATA PRIVACY AND PROTECTION
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

1.1 Applicability
ACME collects and processes personal data about customers, employees, and business partners. This chapter applies to all employees, contractors, and third parties who handle personal data on ACME's behalf. ACME operates under GDPR (for EU/EEA residents), CCPA (California), PIPEDA (Canada), and LGPD (Brazil), among other applicable data protection laws.

1.2 Categories of Personal Data
ACME classifies personal data into three sensitivity tiers:
- Tier 1 (General): Name, business email, job title, publicly available professional information
- Tier 2 (Sensitive): Home address, personal phone, financial account details, performance records, compensation
- Tier 3 (Special Category): Health/medical information, biometric data, racial or ethnic origin, political opinions, criminal records

Special Category data (Tier 3) requires explicit consent from the data subject and must be approved by the Data Protection Officer (DPO) before collection. Contact dpo@acmecorp.com for all Tier 3 data requests.

1.3 Data Subject Rights
Under applicable laws, individuals have the right to:
- Access: Receive a copy of their personal data within 30 days of request (GDPR) or 45 days (CCPA)
- Correction: Have inaccurate data corrected within 30 days
- Deletion (Right to be Forgotten): Have data deleted unless retention is required by law or legitimate business interest
- Portability: Receive data in a machine-readable format
- Opt-Out of Sale: California residents may opt out of sale of their data to third parties

All data subject requests must be routed to privacy@acmecorp.com within 24 hours of receipt. Do not attempt to handle these requests independently.

1.4 Data Retention
Personal data must not be retained longer than necessary for the stated purpose. Standard retention schedules:
- Customer transaction records: 7 years (tax and audit requirements)
- Employee records: 7 years post-separation (most categories), or as required by employment law
- Marketing contact data: 3 years from last engagement, or until opt-out
- Website analytics: 24 months
- CCTV/physical access logs: 90 days

Permanent deletion must be verified and logged. Data stored in backup systems must also be deleted per the backup retention schedule.

1.5 Data Breach Response
Any suspected or confirmed data breach must be reported to securityincident@acmecorp.com AND the DPO immediately — within 1 hour of discovery. Under GDPR, ACME must notify the relevant supervisory authority within 72 hours of discovering a breach that poses a risk to individuals. Failure to report within this window can result in fines up to 4% of global annual turnover. Do not attempt to investigate or remediate a breach independently; follow the Incident Response Playbook on the intranet.

CHAPTER 2: ANTI-MONEY LAUNDERING (AML) AND FINANCIAL CRIMES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

2.1 Overview
ACME is obligated under the Bank Secrecy Act (BSA), the USA PATRIOT Act, and OFAC regulations to maintain an effective Anti-Money Laundering (AML) program. All employees who handle financial transactions, onboard customers, or have access to customer financial data must complete AML training annually.

2.2 Know Your Customer (KYC)
Before establishing a new business relationship involving financial transactions, ACME must verify the identity of the customer or counterparty. Required documentation:
- Individuals: Government-issued photo ID, proof of address, source of funds declaration for transactions above $10,000
- Entities: Certificate of incorporation, beneficial ownership disclosure (all owners >25% stake), and an authorized signatory certificate

Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD) is required for:
- Customers classified as Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs)
- Transactions involving high-risk jurisdictions (see ACME Sanctioned Countries List on compliance portal)
- Unusual transaction patterns flagged by the monitoring system

2.3 Suspicious Activity Reporting
Employees must report suspicious activity to the AML Compliance team immediately (aml@acmecorp.com). Suspicious indicators include:
- Structuring: Multiple transactions just below reporting thresholds ($10,000 for cash)
- Unusual wire transfers to or from sanctioned or high-risk countries
- Customers who are reluctant to provide KYC documentation
- Transactions inconsistent with a customer's stated business purpose
- Round-dollar amounts with no business explanation

NEVER tip off a customer that a Suspicious Activity Report (SAR) is being filed. Disclosure of a SAR to anyone outside the compliance team is a federal crime.

2.4 OFAC Sanctions Compliance
ACME must not conduct business with individuals, entities, or countries on the OFAC Specially Designated Nationals (SDN) list or subject to comprehensive sanctions (currently: Cuba, Iran, North Korea, Syria, Russia - Crimea/Donetsk/Luhansk regions). All new business relationships and transactions above $5,000 must be screened against the OFAC SDN list using the automated screening tool in Salesforce before processing. The screening system is updated automatically. Manual overrides require written sign-off from the General Counsel.

CHAPTER 3: ANTI-BRIBERY AND ANTI-CORRUPTION
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

3.1 FCPA and UK Bribery Act
ACME is subject to the U.S. Foreign Corrupt Practices Act (FCPA) and the UK Bribery Act 2010. These laws prohibit offering, paying, receiving, or authorizing bribes to or from any person — including private individuals and government officials — in order to obtain or retain business or any improper advantage.

3.2 Prohibited Conduct
The following are prohibited regardless of amount or local custom:
- Cash payments to government officials or their family members
- Payments to expedite routine government actions ("facilitation payments") — these are prohibited under ACME policy even where permitted by local law
- Charitable donations made at the request of a government official in connection with business
- Hiring relatives of government officials to influence a decision

3.3 Permissible Hospitality
Reasonable, transparent, and properly documented business entertainment is permitted where it:
- Has a legitimate business purpose
- Is proportionate and not extravagant
- Does not violate the recipient's own employer's policies
- Is properly recorded in the expense system

Hospitality above $200 per person requires pre-approval from your regional compliance officer. Hospitality involving government officials above $75 per person always requires Legal pre-approval.

3.4 Third-Party Due Diligence
ACME may be held liable for bribery committed by agents, distributors, consultants, or JV partners acting on our behalf. Before engaging any third party:
- Screen the party against anti-corruption databases (run through Procurement)
- Conduct risk-based due diligence (questionnaire for low-risk, full background check for high-risk)
- Include anti-bribery representations and warranties in contracts

CHAPTER 4: INSIDER TRADING AND SECURITIES COMPLIANCE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

4.1 Material Non-Public Information (MNPI)
Employees may have access to material non-public information (MNPI) about ACME or its business partners. MNPI is information that: (a) has not been disclosed to the public, and (b) a reasonable investor would consider significant in making an investment decision. Examples include: unannounced earnings, M&A discussions, new product launches, regulatory approvals or actions, and major customer wins or losses.

4.2 Trading Restrictions
Employees in possession of MNPI must NOT trade in ACME securities or the securities of any company to which the MNPI relates. This applies to all security types: shares, options, warrants, bonds, and derivatives. Employees subject to trading restrictions include: all officers, directors, and employees in Finance, Legal, Strategy, Product, and Investor Relations, plus anyone specifically notified by Legal.

4.3 Pre-Clearance and Blackout Periods
Designated employees must obtain pre-clearance from the General Counsel before trading. ACME enforces blackout periods during which no trading is permitted:
- 2 weeks before through 2 trading days after quarterly earnings releases
- During active M&A discussions (notified individually)
Additional ad hoc blackout periods may be imposed at any time. The trading window is approximately 8 weeks per year total. Violations are reported to the SEC and result in immediate termination.

CHAPTER 5: WHISTLEBLOWER AND SPEAK-UP POLICY
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

5.1 Reporting Channels
ACME encourages employees, contractors, and third parties to report suspected violations of law, regulation, or company policy. Reporting channels:
- ACME Ethics Hotline: 1-800-555-ETHIC (anonymous, 24/7, third-party operated)
- Web: acme.ethicspoint.com (anonymous option available)
- Email: ethics@acmecorp.com (non-anonymous)
- Direct contact with the Chief Compliance Officer or General Counsel

5.2 Non-Retaliation
ACME strictly prohibits retaliation against any employee who, in good faith, reports a potential violation. Retaliation includes: demotion, termination, reduced hours, negative performance reviews, exclusion from meetings, or any other adverse employment action taken in connection with a report. Any employee who retaliates against a reporter will be subject to immediate termination.

5.3 Confidentiality
Reports are treated as confidential to the extent possible. Anonymous reporters are protected; however, investigations may uncover identities through circumstantial evidence. ACME cannot guarantee anonymity but will not actively seek to identify anonymous reporters.

5.4 False Reports
Good faith reports, even if ultimately incorrect, are protected. However, reports made knowingly false and with malicious intent are a serious disciplinary offense.

CHAPTER 6: RECORDS MANAGEMENT AND RETENTION
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

6.1 Record-Keeping Obligations
ACME must maintain accurate books and records as required by SEC regulations (for public companies), IRS rules, and various industry regulations. No employee may: falsify records, alter or destroy records outside the retention schedule, or interfere with any audit or investigation.

6.2 Legal Hold
When litigation or a regulatory investigation is anticipated or active, a legal hold ("litigation hold") will be issued by Legal. Employees subject to a legal hold must preserve ALL potentially relevant records — regardless of the standard retention schedule — until the hold is lifted. Destruction of records under a legal hold is obstruction of justice.

6.3 Document Classification
- Public: May be shared externally without restriction
- Internal: For ACME use only; do not share externally
- Confidential: Need-to-know basis; requires encryption in transit and at rest
- Restricted: Highest sensitivity; encrypted, access-logged, executive sign-off required to share

All documents should be labeled upon creation. Unlabeled documents default to "Internal."

CHAPTER 7: THIRD-PARTY AND VENDOR COMPLIANCE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

7.1 Vendor Onboarding Requirements
All vendors who access ACME systems, receive personal data, or provide services with a material compliance risk must complete ACME's vendor due diligence process. This includes: financial stability checks, sanctions screening, information security assessment (ISO 27001 or SOC 2 preferred), and ESG questionnaire for strategic suppliers.

7.2 Data Processing Agreements (DPAs)
Any vendor that processes personal data on ACME's behalf must sign a Data Processing Agreement before data is shared. DPAs must include: purpose and scope limitations, security requirements, sub-processor restrictions, breach notification timelines (within 24 hours), and data deletion commitments post-contract. Template DPAs are available from Legal.

7.3 Ongoing Monitoring
High-risk and strategic vendors are reviewed annually. Compliance monitoring includes: annual SOC 2 report review, penetration test results upon request, adverse news screening quarterly, and incident notification tracking. Vendors who fail monitoring reviews must submit a remediation plan within 30 days or face contract suspension.
