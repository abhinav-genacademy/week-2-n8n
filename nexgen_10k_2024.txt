ITEM 1. BUSINESS

Overview
NexGen Financial Technologies, Inc. ("NexGen," "the Company," "we," "us," or "our") is a technology-driven financial services company that provides embedded finance infrastructure to mid-market and enterprise businesses across North America and Western Europe. Our platform enables non-financial companies to offer their customers banking, lending, and payment services under their own brand — commonly referred to as Banking-as-a-Service (BaaS).

Founded in 2017 and headquartered in Austin, Texas, NexGen went public in April 2021 at $18 per share. As of December 31, 2024, our platform processed $47.2 billion in annualized payment volume and served 312 enterprise clients, up from 241 clients as of December 31, 2023.

Business Segments
We operate through two reportable business segments:

1. Platform Services (72% of 2024 revenue): API-based infrastructure including card issuance, account ledgering, real-time payments (RTP), ACH processing, and Know Your Customer (KYC) / Anti-Money Laundering (AML) compliance modules. Revenue is primarily transaction-fee based, charged as a percentage of payment volume or a flat per-transaction fee.

2. Lending Enablement (28% of 2024 revenue): White-label lending infrastructure that allows our clients to offer Buy Now Pay Later (BNPL), revenue-based financing, and working capital loans to their business or consumer customers. NexGen acts as the technology provider; lending is underwritten by our partner bank, First Commerce Bank, N.A., under a Banking-as-a-Service agreement. Revenue recognized as origination fees (60%), servicing fees (25%), and data licensing (15%).

Competitive Strengths
- Regulatory Relationships: NexGen holds Money Transmitter Licenses (MTLs) in all 50 U.S. states and Canada, a significant barrier to entry that took 4 years to complete.
- Bank Partnership: Exclusive 5-year BaaS agreement with First Commerce Bank (expires December 2027, renewal options available) providing FDIC-insured deposit accounts and lending capacity.
- Enterprise Client Base: Average contract value of $2.4 million annually; average client tenure of 3.8 years. Net Revenue Retention (NRR) of 118% in 2024.
- Technology: Proprietary cloud-native ledger system (U.S. Patent No. 11,423,891) with sub-100ms transaction processing and 99.97% uptime over the past 24 months.

Competition
We compete primarily with Marqeta, Inc., Stripe Treasury, Unit Finance, and Synctera in the BaaS platform market, and with Affirm Holdings and PayPal's BNPL products in the lending enablement market. We also face competition from in-house solutions built by large financial institutions. We believe our differentiation lies in our full-stack regulatory compliance infrastructure, which competitors that lack MTL portfolios cannot easily replicate.

ITEM 1A. RISK FACTORS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Summary of Key Risk Factors (select)

Regulatory and Compliance Risks
- BaaS regulations are evolving rapidly. The OCC, FDIC, and Federal Reserve have each issued guidance in 2023-2024 indicating increased scrutiny of bank-fintech partnerships. Material changes to the regulatory framework could require significant restructuring of our business model.
- We rely on First Commerce Bank as our primary bank partner. If First Commerce's charter were revoked, or if our BaaS agreement is terminated (either party may terminate for material breach with 90 days' notice, or for regulatory direction with 30 days' notice), our ability to offer FDIC-insured products would be severely disrupted.
- A failure in our AML/KYC compliance program could result in regulatory fines, loss of MTLs, and reputational damage. We have invested $18.3 million in compliance technology and personnel since 2022, but no assurance can be given that our program is sufficient.

Financial and Credit Risks
- Our Lending Enablement segment depends on the credit performance of loans originated under our white-label program. While we do not hold loans on our balance sheet, deteriorating credit performance could reduce origination volumes, trigger adverse covenants with First Commerce, and reduce servicing fees.
- Net charge-off rates on loans originated through our platform were 3.2% in 2024, up from 2.7% in 2023, primarily driven by consumer BNPL loans in the 18-24 age demographic.
- We have $125 million outstanding under our 2023 revolving credit facility (Citibank N.A., $200 million total commitment). The facility contains financial covenants including minimum liquidity ($40 million), maximum net leverage (4.0x), and minimum interest coverage (2.5x). A violation of these covenants could result in acceleration of the outstanding balance.

Technology and Operational Risks
- A security breach or data loss event could expose us to regulatory penalties, litigation, and client attrition. Our customer data includes sensitive personal financial information protected under GLBA, CCPA, and GDPR.
- Our platform's uptime commitment to clients is 99.9% per SLA. During Q2 2024, we experienced a 4-hour outage due to a third-party cloud provider incident that resulted in SLA credits of $1.2 million.

Financial Highlights for Fiscal Year 2024
- Total Revenue: $284.6 million, up 31.4% year-over-year (2023: $216.7 million)
- Gross Profit: $171.2 million, gross margin 60.2% (2023: 57.8%)
- Adjusted EBITDA: $42.3 million, Adjusted EBITDA margin 14.9% (2023: 10.2%)
- GAAP Net Loss: ($23.1 million) compared to ($41.8 million) in 2023
- Total Payment Volume (TPV): $47.2 billion, up 44% year-over-year
- Enterprise Clients: 312 as of December 31, 2024 (2023: 241)
- Net Revenue Retention (NRR): 118% (2023: 112%)

Revenue Discussion
Platform Services revenue grew 28.1% to $205.1 million in 2024. Transaction volume growth of 44% was partially offset by price compression of approximately 8% due to competitive pressure and volume discount commitments made to three large enterprise clients during 2023 renewals. We expect moderate continued price compression of 3-5% annually through 2026.

Lending Enablement revenue grew 40.7% to $79.5 million in 2024, driven by the expansion of our BNPL offering into 12 new enterprise client platforms. Origination volume reached $3.1 billion in 2024 (2023: $2.0 billion). However, rising net charge-offs (3.2% vs. 2.7% in 2023) led management to tighten underwriting criteria in Q4 2024. We expect origination growth to moderate to 15-20% in 2025 as a result.

Operating Expenses
Total operating expenses were $307.7 million in 2024 (2023: $258.5 million). The increase was driven by: headcount growth (612 to 847 employees, +38%), increased stock-based compensation ($34.1 million vs. $27.6 million in 2023), and $22.8 million in one-time charges related to our restructuring initiative in Q3 2024 (elimination of 47 positions in G&A and legacy infrastructure).

Research and development expense was $78.2 million (27.5% of revenue), reflecting ongoing investment in our next-generation ledger platform (Project Horizon, expected GA in Q2 2025) and AI-driven credit risk models.

Liquidity and Capital Resources
As of December 31, 2024:
- Cash and cash equivalents: $187.4 million
- Short-term investments: $43.2 million
- Outstanding revolving credit facility: $125 million
- Net cash position (cash minus revolver): $105.6 million

Free cash flow for FY2024: $18.7 million, compared to ($12.3 million) in FY2023. We expect to achieve positive free cash flow on a full-year basis in 2025. Capital expenditures were $8.1 million in 2024, primarily data center and hardware refresh. We do not anticipate significant capex needs in 2025 given our cloud-native architecture.

Guidance for Fiscal Year 2025
Management provides the following guidance for fiscal year 2025:
- Total Revenue: $355 - $370 million (25-30% growth)
- Adjusted EBITDA: $62 - $68 million (17-18.5% margin)
- Enterprise Clients: 370 - 390
- Free Cash Flow: Positive for the full year
